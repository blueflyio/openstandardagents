# Academia and Educational Research (2025-2026)

Reference date for relative time normalization: **March 10, 2026**.

## Cornell

eCornell's **Agentic AI Architecture** certificate is explicitly structured from fundamentals to production concerns:

- LLM behavior, context engineering, and hallucination limits
- RAG for trusted/private data grounding
- multi-agent workflows and protocol/handoff patterns
- governance, risk, security, and human oversight [SRC-23]

This is notable because the curriculum sequence mirrors the real production stack: model quality -> orchestration -> protocol interoperability -> governance.

## Harvard

### Library Innovation Lab / Berkman context

Harvard Library Innovation Lab's **Agent Protocols Tech Tree (APTT)** frames open protocols as the practical coordination surface for fast-moving agent ecosystems. The post ties current protocol work to historical internet protocol standardization patterns (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP). [SRC-24]

The same material stresses that agent builders can move faster than formal regulation, making protocol design a de facto governance mechanism in the near term. [SRC-24]

### Policy scholarship around an "agentic web"

Harvard-affiliated policy/legal discussions raise governance concerns around rapidly growing autonomous traffic and institutional accountability on the web. [SRC-27][SRC-28]

## MIT

### MIT AI Agent Index (2025)

The MIT index reports:

- accelerating releases and autonomy (including browser-agent L4-L5 behavior bands)
- significant safety/transparency asymmetry (capability disclosure exceeds safety disclosure)
- limited internal and third-party safety result publication by many vendors [SRC-25]

It also flags unsettled web-conduct norms (for example, inconsistent behavior around robots.txt and anti-bot boundaries). [SRC-25]

### MIT Sloan (decision guidance for 2026)

MIT Sloan's guidance describes a near-term paradox:

- adoption pressure is high
- hallucinations and prompt-injection risk still constrain readiness
- many firms still need human-in-the-loop controls
- despite this, expert forecasts still expect broad transaction handling by agents within approximately five years [SRC-26]

## Other academic and research sources

- **ACM/ACL/ICML-adjacent security papers** (AGENTVIGIL, MELON) and related benchmarks (WASP) continue to show practical prompt-injection risk and evaluate defensive controls. [SRC-41][SRC-42][SRC-43]
- These papers reinforce institutional findings: capability growth is outpacing standardized safety evidence in production settings.

## Academic takeaways

1. Education is shifting from "prompting" to **systems architecture + governance**.
2. The strongest university themes are **interoperability, accountability, and verifiable controls**, not just model performance.
3. In 2026 planning, institutions increasingly treat agent deployment as a **socio-technical governance problem**.
